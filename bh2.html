Airbnb, downtown
Back to the rhymes

It was my return to Belo Horizonte, for another National Battle. A year earlier, I had walked those same neighborhoods. BH was no longer unfamiliar, but each trip writes its own kind of chaos, and this one was no exception. The 2024 Nationals, its 12th edition, came with a new format: two days of event. On Saturday, a sort of press conference with the selected MCs, some battles, and lots of breakdancing. On Sunday, the big showdown.

I left Uberlândia on a Thursday night, straight from work, still wearing my school uniform. On the bus, I sat next to a woman who, after a stop, asked if I was a teacher. We started talking, and before we knew it, we had spent the night chatting. Her name was Ana. We even found out we had a student in common. It felt like fate had given me another last-minute travel company.

We arrived in BH Friday morning. This time, I had booked an Airbnb right downtownwn — perfect location.

At 9 a.m., there was a protest scheduled for the end of the 6x1 work schedule. I made sure to be there, even delayed my trip just to join the manifestation. Fighting for causes is part of my baggage too. I even joined Unidade Popular that day.
